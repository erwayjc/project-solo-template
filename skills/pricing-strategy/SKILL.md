## Pricing Strategy Playbook

### Pricing Frameworks

**Value-Based Pricing**
- Price based on the outcome/transformation delivered, not time spent
- Ask: "What is this result worth to the customer?"
- Digital products should be priced at 10-20% of the value they create

**Tiered Pricing** (recommended for courses/memberships)
- **Starter**: Core content only — entry point for price-sensitive buyers
- **Professional**: Core + bonuses + community — the "most popular" tier
- **Premium**: Everything + 1:1 access — high-touch, high-price

**Anchor Pricing**
- Show the highest tier first to anchor value perception
- The middle tier should be the target (most buyers choose it)
- Use crossed-out "original" prices sparingly and honestly

### Price Points (Digital Products)
- **Lead magnets**: Free (email in exchange for value)
- **Mini-courses / workshops**: $27-97
- **Signature courses**: $197-997
- **Premium programs**: $997-2,997
- **High-ticket coaching**: $3,000+

### Discount Strategy
- Launch discounts: 20-30% off for first 48-72 hours
- Never discount more than 40% (devalues the product)
- Use bonuses instead of discounts when possible
- Time-limited offers outperform permanent discounts
- Avoid perpetual sales (trains customers to wait)

### Key Principles
- Odd pricing ($197 vs $200) works for courses under $500
- Round pricing ($500 vs $497) works better for premium/luxury
- Always show price in context of value ("$297 for a system that saves 10 hours/week")
- Include a money-back guarantee to reduce purchase friction
